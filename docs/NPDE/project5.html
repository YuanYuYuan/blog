Finite Difference of 2D Laplacian¶

$$ u_{i,j} = \frac{u_{i-1,j}-2u_{i,j}+u_{i+1,j}}{\Delta x^2} + \frac{u_{i,j-1}-2u_{i,j}+u_{i,j+1}}{\Delta y^2} $$

In the following code, $\Delta x = \Delta y = h$, where $h$ is the grid size.

We can represent discrete Laplacian in a 2D matrix form $$ \frac{1}{h^2}\begin{pmatrix} & 1 & \\ 1 & -4 & 1 \\ & 1 & \end{pmatrix} $$

but to compute $u$ in convenient, we must vectorize $u$, so the discrete Laplacian becomes

$$\frac{1}{h^2} \begin{pmatrix} \ddots & & & & \\ & L & I & & \\ & I & L & I & \\ & & I & L & \\ & & & & \ddots \end{pmatrix} $$

where $I$ is identity matrix and

$$ L = \begin{pmatrix} \ddots & & & & \\ & -4 & 1 & & \\ & 1 & -4 & 1 & \\ & & 1 & -4 & \\ & & & & \ddots \end{pmatrix} $$

In [2]:
def Laplacian_matrix(N):
    L = sparse.diags([1, -4, 1], [-1, 0, 1], shape=(N, N))
    I = sparse.eye(N)
    J = sparse.diags([1, 0, 1], [-1, 0, 1], shape=(N, N))
    return sparse.kron(I, L) + sparse.kron(J, I)

Restriction in 2D¶

Using full weighting restriction,

$$ u^{2h}_{ij} = sum \begin{pmatrix} 1/16 & 1/8 & 1/16 \\ 1/8 & 1/4 & 1/8 \\ 1/16 & 1/8 & 1/16 \end{pmatrix} \odot \begin{pmatrix} u^{h}_{i-1j+1} & u^{h}_{ij+1} & u^{h}_{i+1j+1} \\ u^{h}_{i-1j} & u^{h}_{ij} & u^{h}_{i+1j} \\ u^{h}_{i-1j-1} & u^{h}_{ij-1} & u^{h}_{i+1j-1} \end{pmatrix} $$

For computaional convenience, we vectorize it and create a restriction operator

$$ I^{2h}_{h} = \begin{pmatrix} \ddots & & & & & & \\ & \frac{ M}{2} & M & \frac{ M}{2} & & & \\ & & & \frac{M}{2} & M & \frac{ M}{2} & \\ & & & & & & \ddots \end{pmatrix} $$

where

$$ M = \begin{pmatrix} \ddots & & & & & & \\ & \frac{1}{8} & \frac{ 1}{4} & \frac{1}{8} & & & \\ & & & \frac{1}{8} & \frac{ 1}{4} & \frac{ 1}{8} & \\ & & & & & & \ddots \end{pmatrix} $$

In [4]:
def restriction_matrix(grid_level):
    N = 2**grid_level - 1
    coarse_N = 2**(grid_level-1) - 1
    I = sparse.csr_matrix((coarse_N, N))
    for i in range(coarse_N):
        I[i, i*2] = .5
        I[i, i*2+1] = 1
        I[i, i*2+2] = .5
    M = I / 4
    return sparse.kron(I, M)

Relaxation: Use Jaccobi Method¶

To solve a linear equaion:

$$ A u = f $$

first divide $A$ into diagonal part $D$ and the other part $R$,

and use the following iteration method to approximate the solution.

$$ u^{(n+1)} = u^{(n)} + D^{-1} (f - A u^{(n)}) $$

In [6]:
def Jaccobi_method(A, f, N=10, u0=None):
    x = np.zeros(f.shape) if (u0 is None) else u0
    D = A.diagonal()
    R = A - sparse.diags(D)
    for i in range(N):
        x = (f - R @ x) / D.reshape(-1, 1)
    return x

Prolongation: Use Linear Interpolation¶

http://www.cs.huji.ac.il/~csip/CSIP2007-MG.pdf

Note that we can directly compute prolongation matrix by the following relation

$$ I^{h}_{2h} = 4 (I^{2h}_{h})^T $$

In the following code, the notation is

$$ P = 4 * R^T $$

V-Cycle¶

In [7]:
def vcycle(A, f, u=None):
    grid_level = np.log2(np.sqrt(f.shape[0])+1)
    assert (grid_level - int(grid_level)) == 0
    grid_level = int(grid_level)

    least_level = 1
    if grid_level <= least_level:
        return spsolve(A, f).reshape(-1, 1)

    if u is None:
        u = np.zeros(f.shape)

    # pre smoothing
    v = Jaccobi_method(A, f, N=5, u0=u)

    # compute residual
    residual = f - A @ v

    # project residual onto coarser mesh(next level)
    R = restriction_matrix(grid_level)
    coarse_residual = R @ residual

    # prolongatation matrix
    P = 4 * R.T

    # solve coarse grid equation
    coarse_A = R @ A @ P
    coarse_v = vcycle(coarse_A, coarse_residual)

    # correction/prolongation
    v += P @ coarse_v

    # post smoothing
    u = Jaccobi_method(A, f, N=5, u0=v)

    return u

Set Grid and Initialize Laplacian Matrix¶

In [8]:
def initialize(grid_level):
    N = 2**grid_level+1
    h = 1/2**grid_level
    partition = np.linspace(0, 1, N)
    x, y = np.meshgrid(partition, partition)
    A = Laplacian_matrix(N-2)/h**2
    return x, y, A

Apply multigrid method to approximate¶

In [12]:
def solve(grid_level, test_function, tolerance=1e-5, max_iteration=20, verbose=False):
    
    start = timeit.default_timer()
    
    x, y, A = initialize(grid_level)
    f, exact_u = test_function(x, y)
    
    # choose values in boundary
    exact_u = exact_u[1:-1, 1:-1]
    f = f[1:-1, 1:-1]
    
    # vectorize the matrix to a vector
    exact_u = exact_u.flatten('F').reshape(-1, 1)
    f = f.flatten('F').reshape(-1, 1)

    # itertation step
    u = np.zeros(f.shape)
    for i in range(max_iteration):
        u = vcycle(A, f, u)
        # absolute max error
        err = np.max(np.abs(u-exact_u))
        if verbose:
            print("iter %d, err = %e" % (i+1, err))
        if err < tolerance:
            break
            
    # compute spent time
    time = timeit.default_timer() - start
    
    return i+1, err, time
